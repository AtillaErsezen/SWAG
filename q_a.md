## 🏗️ Technical Specifications (Numerical Data)

### 1. Rail Form Weight
**Question:** What is the exact weight per meter for a 60 E2 rail form?
> **Answer:** 60.03 kg/m.

### 2. Electrical Sleeper Resistance
**Question:** What is the minimum ballast resistance required during the measurement of electrical sleeper resistance for concrete sleepers?
> **Answer:** 3 ohm/km.

### 3. Prestressed Concrete Sleeper Dimensions
**Question:** What are the standard dimensions for a prestressed concrete sleeper B 70 W?
> **Answer:** 
> - **Length:** 2600 mm
> - **Width (at base):** 300 mm
> - **Height:** 210 mm

## 📜 Regulatory & Compliance (The "Rules")

### 4. Insulated Joint Usage
**Question:** According to the manual, when is it permissible to use design type MT insulated joints instead of the standard IVB 30°?
> **Answer:** Only if factory-manufactured IVB 30° joints cannot be used, such as for structural joints of switches and crossings.

### 5. High-Speed Line Constraints
**Question:** Are wooden sleepers permitted for use on high-speed lines exceeding 160 km/h?
> **Answer:** No, they are unsuitable because they exhibit 15% lower lateral displacement resistance compared to standard concrete alternatives.

### 7. Y-Steel Sleeper Disadvantages
**Question:** What are the primary disadvantages of using the Y-steel sleeper St 98 compared to standard sleepers?
> **Answer:** More complex maintenance requirements and the necessity for specialized switch tamping machines.

## 🧪 Modern Technology (Ballastless Systems)

### 8. Elastic Padding Materials
**Question:** Which elastic materials have proved suitable for 'padding' concrete sleepers to reduce ballast stress on bridges or tunnels?
> **Answer:** Sylomer and Sylodyn.

### 9. RHEDA 2000 System Components
**Question:** Identify the concrete sleeper design type used specifically for the RHEDA 2000 ballastless track system.
> **Answer:** Twin-block concrete sleeper B 355.3 W60M.
